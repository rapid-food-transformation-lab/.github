# Rapid Food Transformation Lab

**Oxford Martin School, University of Oxford**

We study how food, energy, and economic systems must transform to avoid the worst climate outcomes. Led by Professor Paul Behrens, our research combines environmental footprint analysis, input-output modelling, and systems approaches to understand both the scale of change required and the pathways that can deliver it.

Food systems are responsible for roughly a third of global greenhouse gas emissions, consume the majority of the world's arable land, and drive most biodiversity loss. Transforming them is not optional. Our work quantifies what transformation looks like across supply chains, diets, land use, and trade — and what it means for energy systems, livelihoods, and economic stability.

## What you will find here

This GitHub Organisation hosts the computational work of the lab. We use a flat repository structure: each project gets one repo (private during WIP, public on submission), alongside a small set of permanent shared infrastructure repos.

## Open science

We treat code as a research output. On publication, analysis repositories become public, fully documented, and structured for reproducibility. All repos follow a consistent scaffold — standardised directory layout, versioned datasets with checksums, and a `CLAUDE.md` file at the root that makes the repository legible to both humans and AI-assisted tools. Large datasets are archived on Zenodo, or other online archives, rather than committed to git.
